## 📊 Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic

**Tool used:** Canva

For this task I created a one page digital literacy awareness infographic using Canva. The goal was to present important information in a way that is visually engaging and easy for my batchmates to understand at a glance, rather then just reading through a block of text.

**The infographic covered three key topics:**
- 📖 What digital literacy is and why it matters for students
- 🔒 Safe internet practices like using strong passwords and avoiding suspicious links
- ✉️ Email etiquette including proper greetings, subject lines and professional language

I chose Canva specifically because its very beginner friendly and has a lot of ready made templates that made the whole designing process much more manageable for me. I had used Canva once or twice before but never really for something this structured, so it was a good learning experience.

The biggest challenge I faced was trying to keep the content concise while also making sure the layout looked clean and readable. Its honestly harder then it sounds to balance good design with meaningful content, but I am fairly happy with how the final infographic turned out.

## 📧 Task 4 – Email Etiquette & Responsible Communication

For this task I focused on professional email communication and responsible social media use, two things that I think are genuinely very important for college students but often get overlooked.

**Emails drafted:**
- 📩 A formal email requesting an assignment deadline extension from a professor
- 📩 A formal email expressing interest in a summer internship opportunity at a company

**While writing these emails I made sure to focus on:**
- A clear and specific subject line
- A respectful and professional greeting
- A well organised body that clearly explains the purpose
- A proper sign off with full name

Working on this task made me realise how much of a difference proper email structure makes in how professional you come across. A lot of students, including myself honestly, tend to send very casual emails to professors without realising how unprofessional it can look.

I also created a **Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist** as part of this task, which has five Do's and five Don'ts for responsible social media use as a college student. It was a good exercise to actually think about digital behaviour more consciously.

📁 `task-4-email-etiquette/`

## 🔐 Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness

**Topic researched:** UPI Fraud

For the final task I researched UPI fraud which is currently one of the most widespread and rapidly growing forms of cybercrime in India, especially among college students and young adults.

**The case study covers:**
- 💸 What UPI fraud is and how it typically happens step by step
- 🎯 Who is usually targeted and why they are vulnerable
- ⚠️ The consequences for victims including financial loss and mental stress

What genuinely surprised me the most during this research was how convincing and sophisticated these scammers actually are. I always assumed most people would be able to spot a scam call pretty easily, but after reading about how these frauds actually work I realised that even educated and careful individuals can fall for them without realising it. That was honestly a bit of an eye opener for me.

I also put together a **Stay Safe Online prevention checklist** which includes:
- ✅ 8+ specific and actionable prevention tips for college students in India
- ✅ At least two tips specifically around UPI and financial safety
- ✅ Reporting channels including the **National Cyber Crime Portal** — cybercrime.gov.in and helpline **1930**

📁 `task-5-cybercrime/`
